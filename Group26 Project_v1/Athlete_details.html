Eileen Gu

Eileen Gu, is an American-born freestyle skier, two-time Olympic gold medalist, and model. She has competed for China in halfpipe, slopestyle, and big air events since 2019.

Biography

GU Ailing Eileen is one of those people who seems to be good at everything. From winning three freestyle skiing medals (including two golds) at the Lausanne 2020 Youth Olympic Games, to gracing the front cover of Vogue Magazine, the part-time model is also an excellent student who is planning to go to Stanford University.
Aged 18, she became the first woman to land a forward double cork 1440 and is a rare ‘triple-threat’ in the three freeski events of half pipe, big air, and slopestyle. She’s also fluent in both English and Mandarin. With such a busy life, the Chinese two-time World Championship gold medallist in 2021 likes to chill out by cooking, especially dumplings with her grandmother. One of Gu's favourite ingredients? Truffle oil.

Career

In 2021, Gu became the first woman to land a forward double cork 1440.

X Games

At the 2021 Winter X Games, Gu won a bronze medal in Big Air and two gold medals in SuperPipe and Slopestyle, becoming the first rookie to win a gold medal in Women's Ski SuperPipe, the first rookie to medal in three events, and the first athlete representing China to win a gold medal at the X Games.

World Championships

Gu competed at the FIS Freestyle Ski and Snowboarding World Championships 2021, winning two gold medals in Freeski Halfpipe and Freeski Slopestyle and a bronze medal in Freeski Big Air. Gu became the first freeskier to win two golds at the FIS Freeski World Championship.[33][34] She competed without poles for the first time due to a broken hand, having fractured a finger and tearing the UCL in her thumb.

2022 Winter Olympics

At the 2022 Winter Olympics, Gu became the youngest gold medalist in freestyle skiing, winning the big air event.[38] Gu landed her first attempt at a double cork 1620, which she had never tried in competition.[39] She was the second woman to land the trick and the first woman to land a left-turn 1620;[40] Tess Ledeux first successfully completed a double cork 1620 on 21 January 2022 at the X Games in Aspen, Colorado [41] and landed it again in her first run of the big air final at the 2022 Winter Olympics.[

Gu won the silver medal in the slopestyle event.[44] She won a second gold medal in the women's freeski halfpipe competition, becoming the first freestyle skier to win three medals at a Winter Olympics.
